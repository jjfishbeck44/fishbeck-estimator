## Cost drivers (raise the price)
Rock/difficult soil, environmental permits, limited site access, premium materials
(copper/slate/tile, high-strength or stamped concrete, custom millwork), complex
layouts, steep roof slopes, high ceilings requiring scaffolding, Level 5 drywall
finish, cold-weather protection, extensive surface prep.

## Cost reducers (lower the price)
Large/simple/open areas, standard materials and sizes, good site/equipment access,
repetitive layouts (multi-unit), new construction (no tear-off), bulk purchasing,
off-season scheduling (winter in northern climates).

## Regional variation
High-cost markets (SF Bay, NYC, Seattle, Boston, DC): +10–40% over national avg.
Low-cost markets (rural Midwest, parts of TX/OK/AR, rural Southeast): −15–30%.
Twin Cities sits near the national average — use these ranges as the baseline.

## Bidding guidance
- Contingency: **5–8% renovation**, **3–5% new construction**.
- Firm up major material packages with supplier quotes before bidding.
- Escalation clauses on projects over 6 months.

## Disclaimer
2024 general market ranges for **preliminary budgeting only**. Always confirm with
current local supplier/subcontractor quotes for the specific project scope, size,
timing, and location before any contractual commitment.
